Courses

We keep our classes small to give maximum attention to each individual's needs. All classes follow an immersion approach with English only used when needed. We work on developing all the communication and comprehension skills.
Classes cater for varied students:
• Those coming from bi-/multilingual families where they understand but cannot or are not confident enough to speak a target language
• Those who are interested in a selected target language and have never been exposed to it
• Those who understand and speak a little of a target language and are looking to improve their proficiency

Rera Language School offers fun-filled and practical lessons and attention is paid to individual needs.

"Just learning to think in another language allows you to see your own culture in a better viewpoint"
- Gates McFadden

Our Methods

Our classes are fun-filled for children, teens and adults to motivate, engage and immerse all students. We use role plays, songs, dance, games, storytelling as well as arts and craft original music, dance, games and storytelling.

Our key goal is to build communicative skills of speaking and listening, reading and writing, applying techniques of total physical response. This approach is adapted for each age group.

Children's classes

Our child and teenage students differ from each other but have the same goal of learning or improving on a target African language. Our younger students include:
• Bi-/multi-lingual families living in Johannesburg where children are not immersed in their heritage language(s) and have minimal practice outside their homes
• Children and teens who have not been exposed to an African language but are interested in learning
• Children requiring extra tutoring support for an African language they are learning at school

There are many reasons for children to learn a new language:
• For heritage learners, this improves connectivity and communication with extended family
• For all learners, learning a new language helps children interact with people who speak the language.
• Make new friends with people in your selected target African language.
• Gain access to a world of multilingualism
• African languages are everywhere
• Increase your children's knowledge and perceptions of the world
• African languages are fun!

Adult lessons

It is never too late to learn a new language so boost your linguistic universe with a new African language.

There are many reasons for adults to learn a new language:
• Make new friends with people in your selected target African language.
• Understand and sing to songs in your target African language.
• Interact with people who speak the language.
• Exercise your brain, which according to research is a way of averting symptoms of dementia and Alzheimer's.
